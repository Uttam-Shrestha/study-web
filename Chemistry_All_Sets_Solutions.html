Group B: Short Answer Solutions

1. 3N₂H₄ + 4ClO₃⁻ → 6NO + 4Cl⁻ + 6H₂O. OA: ClO₃⁻, RA: N₂H₄.

2. Le-Chatelier: Shifts to undo change. Pressure lowers melting point of ice because ice has larger volume.

3. Rutherford: Most space empty, positive nucleus, revolving electrons.

4. Tritium (fusion fuel), Nascent H (atomic, stronger reducer than H₂).

5. Rhombic sulfur (most stable). Transition temp: 95.5°C.

6. Octane number: 60 (60% iso-octane). Knocking: pre-ignition noise.

7. Aromatic: Naphthalene, Furan, Pyrrole (6/10 π electrons). Anti-aromatic: Cyclobutadiene.

8. Kipp's apparatus: Intermittent H₂S supply via FeS + dil HCl.

Group C: Long Answer Solutions

9. Limiting reagent: O₂. Volume of CO₂: 6.63 L.

10. Ostwald's Process: 4NH₃ + 5O₂ → 4NO + 6H₂O → 2NO₂ → 2HNO₃ + NO.

11. Ethyne → (Hydration) → Acetaldehyde → (Oxidation) → Acetic Acid → (Reduction) → Ethanol/Methanol (Complex).

Group B: Short Answer Solutions

1. EA (energy released adding e-), EN (attracting shared e-). Size: I⁻ > I > I⁺.

2. CuSO₄ electrolysis: 1.48g Cu deposited at cathode.

4. Graham's Law: r1/r2 = √(M2/M1). Unknown gas (r=4x faster than O₂) is H₂ (M=2).

5. HCl Prep: NaCl + H₂SO₄ → NaHSO₄ + HCl. H₂SO₄ oxidizes HBr to Br₂, so not used for HBr.

6. Down's Cell: Molten NaCl + CaCl₂ electrolysis. Na at cathode, Cl₂ at anode.

7. Lassaigne's Test: NaCN + FeSO₄ → Prussian blue indicates Nitrogen.

8. Ozonolysis of P (C₆H₁₂): P is 2,3-dimethyl-but-2-ene giving 2 units of acetone.

9. Al₂O₃/ZnO are amphoteric: react with both HCl and NaOH.

Group C: Long Answer Solutions

1. Mg + H₂SO₄: Mg is limiting. 0.1167 mol H₂SO₄ excess.

2. Contact Process: S → SO₂ → SO₃ (V₂O₅ catalyst) → H₂SO₄.

3. Wolf-Kishner: C=O → CH₂ using NH₂NH₂/KOH.
