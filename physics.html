🧠 “GIVE REASONS” – scientific reasoning + formula proof

1. Projectile follows a parabolic path

Horizontal motion: \(x = u\cos\theta \cdot t\) → \(t = x/(u\cos\theta)\). Vertical: \(y = u\sin\theta\, t - \frac12 g t^2\). Substituting gives \(y = x\tan\theta - \frac{g x^2}{2u^2\cos^2\theta}\). This is a quadratic in \(x\) → parabola.

\(y = (\tan\theta)x - \left(\frac{g}{2u^2\cos^2\theta}\right)x^2\) → standard parabola \(y = ax - bx^2\).

2. Satellite does no work in orbit

Work \(W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d}\). Gravitational force is centripetal, always perpendicular to instantaneous displacement (tangential velocity). For circular orbit, \(\theta = 90^\circ\) → \(\cos90^\circ = 0\) → \(W=0\). Energy conserved.

\(dW = \vec{F}_g \cdot d\vec{r}\) and \(\vec{F}_g \perp d\vec{r}\) → \(dW=0\).

3. Circular motion accelerates even at constant speed

Acceleration is change in velocity vector. Speed constant but direction changes → centripetal acceleration \(a_c = v^2/r\) directed toward centre.

\(\vec{a} = \frac{d\vec{v}}{dt}\); magnitude \(a_c = \frac{v^2}{r}\), always present in uniform circular motion.

4. Steel bowls painted with copper at base

Copper has high thermal conductivity \(k\). Rate of heat transfer \(P = kA\frac{\Delta T}{L}\). Copper base rapidly conducts heat from stove to food, improving efficiency.

\(k_{\text{copper}} \approx 400\,\text{W/m·K}\) vs steel ~\(50\,\text{W/m·K}\).

5. Mud houses are cooler in summer, warmer in winter

Mud has low thermal conductivity and high heat capacity. It resists heat flow (thermal lag) → interior temperature remains stable.

Low \(k\) → smaller \(P\) for same gradient; high specific heat \(c\) → absorbs large heat with small \(\Delta T\).

6. Temperature constant during melting/boiling

Heat supplied is used to overcome intermolecular bonds (potential energy) not to increase kinetic energy. Latent heat \(Q = mL\).

\(Q = mL_f\) or \(mL_v\); no change in \(T\) until phase transition completes.

7. Light bends when entering denser medium

Speed of light decreases in denser medium → Snell’s law: \(n_1\sin i = n_2\sin r\). If \(n_2 > n_1\), \(r < i\) → bends toward normal.

\(v = c/n\); change in wavefront direction follows Fermat’s principle.

8. Two equipotential surfaces never intersect

Each surface has constant potential \(V\). If they intersect, at intersection point potential would have two different values simultaneously → impossible.

If \(V_1 = V_a\) and \(V_2 = V_b\) with \(V_a\neq V_b\) intersect → contradiction.

9. Mass of nucleus < sum of nucleons (mass defect)

Missing mass converted into binding energy \(E_b = \Delta m c^2\) (mass-energy equivalence).

\(\Delta m = Z m_p + (A-Z)m_n - M_{\text{nucleus}}\); \(E_b = \Delta m \cdot c^2\) explains stability.

10. Convex mirrors used in vehicles

Convex mirror provides wider field of view (diverging mirror). Though image is diminished, driver sees larger area for safety.

Field of view ∝ \(1/|f|\) (diverging). The relation \(\frac1f = (n-1)(\frac1{R_1}-\frac1{R_2})\) for convex gives negative \(f\).

11. Absolute zero is not zero energy

At absolute zero (0 K), atoms still have zero‑point energy due to Heisenberg uncertainty principle. Motion never completely stops.

Quantum harmonic oscillator ground state energy \(E_0 = \frac12 \hbar\omega > 0\).

12. Rain streaks slant on car windows

Relative velocity of rain w.r.t car: \(\vec{v}_{r/c} = \vec{v}_r - \vec{v}_c\). Vector sum makes raindrops appear to come at an angle.

If rain vertical \(v_r\) and car horizontal \(v_c\), \(\tan\theta = v_c / v_r\) → slanted streaks.

13. Plane mirror can’t replace convex mirror in cars

Plane mirror gives narrow field of view (reflection law). Convex mirror produces virtual, diminished image but with large angular coverage.

Field of view angle \(\phi\) for plane is limited by mirror size; convex curvature increases \(\phi\).

14. Dimensionally correct equation may be physically incorrect

Example: \(s = ut + at^2\) is dimensionally [L] = [L] + [L] but true equation is \(s = ut + \frac12 at^2\). Missing dimensionless factor changes physics.

Dimensional analysis cannot fix numerical constants; experimental verification needed.

📖 DEFINITIONS (easy marks – memorize)

Projectile motion – motion of an object thrown into space under gravity only.
Conservative force – work done independent of path, depends only on endpoints (e.g., gravity).
Angle of repose – max slope angle where object just begins to slide (\(\mu = \tan\theta\)).
Angle of friction – angle whose tangent equals coefficient of friction (\(\phi = \tan^{-1}\mu\)).
Escape velocity – min velocity to escape planet’s gravity without further propulsion.
Young’s modulus – ratio of tensile stress to tensile strain (\(Y = \frac{FL}{A\Delta L}\)).
Coefficient of linear expansion – fractional change in length per °C (\(\alpha = \frac{\Delta L}{L_0\Delta T}\)).
Specific heat capacity – heat required to raise 1 kg by 1 K (\(c = Q/(m\Delta T)\)).
Latent heat of fusion – heat to change 1 kg solid → liquid at constant \(T\).
Thermal conductivity – rate of heat transfer per unit thickness per unit temp difference (\(k\)).
Critical angle – incidence angle in denser medium giving refraction angle 90°.
Lateral shift – perpendicular distance between incident and emergent ray in glass slab.
Electric potential – work done per unit positive charge from infinity to a point.
Capacitor – device to store electric charge and energy (\(C = Q/V\)).
Binding energy – energy required to disassemble nucleus into nucleons.
Mass defect – difference between mass of nucleus and sum of its nucleon masses.
Impulse – change in momentum (\(J = \Delta p = F \Delta t\)).
Absolute zero – lowest possible temperature (0 K = −273.15 °C), particles have minimum energy.
GPS (Global Positioning System) – satellite‑based navigation using time signals & trilateration.
Hooke’s law – stress ∝ strain within elastic limit.

IIMPORTANT “GIVE REASONS” (short & exam‑ready)

Why a projectile has parabolic path

– Because horizontal motion is uniform and vertical motion is uniformly accelerated, giving y∝x2y∝x2.

Why satellite doesn’t work in orbit

– Force (gravity) is perpendicular to displacement → work = 0.

Why object in circular motion accelerates at constant speed

– Direction changes continuously; acceleration is centripetal.

Why steel bowls painted with copper at base

– Copper has high thermal conductivity, distributes heat quickly.

Why mud houses are cooler in summer and warmer in winter

– Mud has low thermal conductivity and high thermal mass, so it heats/cools slowly.

Why temperature doesn’t rise during melting/boiling

– Supplied heat is used to break intermolecular bonds (latent heat).

Why light bends when entering denser medium

– Speed decreases, causing change in direction (Snell’s law).

Why two equipotential surfaces never intersect

– Intersection would imply two different potentials at same point.

Why mass of nucleus < sum of its nucleons

– Mass defect converted into binding energy.

Why convex mirror used in cars, not plane

– Convex gives wider field of view (though image is smaller).

Why absolute zero is not zero energy

– Particles have zero‑point energy due to quantum uncertainty.

Why rain streaks slant on car windows

– Relative velocity of rain with respect to car is vector sum.

Why plane mirror cannot replace convex mirror

– Plane mirror has limited field of view.

Why a dimensionally correct equation may be physically incorrect

– Example: s=ut+at2s=ut+at2 is dimensionally correct but wrong (missing ½).
